Drawing

Sketching is a hobby I enjoy in my free time.
I like how simple it is—all I need is a pencil and some paper.
When I sketch, I feel relaxed and it helps me take my mind off things.
I usually draw things I see around me, like objects on my desk or scenes from outside my window.
I am not an expert, but I notice that I get a little better each time I practice.

I want to explore more areas of it. So far, I have mostly done simple sketches with pencils, but now I want to try using colors like watercolors and markers to make my drawings more lively.
I’m also interested in learning digital drawing using apps on my tablet or computer. Besides that, I want to practice drawing different subjects like animals, landscapes, and cartoons to improve my skills.
Exploring these new styles and tools will help me become a better artist and keep drawing fun and exciting for me.

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Early Life and Education

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
He came from a humble family and worked hard to get an education.
He studied physics and aerospace engineering.

Career in Science and Technology

After completing his studies, Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in 1958.
Later, he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was the project director for India’s first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
This project successfully placed the Rohini satellite in space in 1980.

Contributions to India’s Missile and Nuclear Programs

Kalam played a key role in developing India’s missile programs and earned the title "Missile Man of India" for his work on missiles like Agni and Prithvi .
He was also a major figure in India’s 1998 nuclear tests, which established India as a nuclear power.

Later Life and Legacy

After his presidency ended in 2007, Kalam continued teaching and inspiring students.
He wrote several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire" .
He received many awards, including the Bharat Ratna.
He passed away on 27 July 2015 while giving a lecture, leaving a lasting legacy.
